## The Black Hole of the Backlog

Every Product Manager knows the feeling: you open your Jira or Linear backlog, and there are 800+ tickets staring back at you. It's a graveyard where good ideas go to die and tech debt piles up endlessly. Traditional backlog grooming sessions take hours of expensive engineering time, and often devolve into subjective arguments over priority. 

We decided to experiment with a Generative AI-driven approach to automate the initial pass of feature prioritization.

![AI Processing Data](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1551288049-bebda4e38f71?auto=format&fit=crop&q=80)

### The Automated Scoring Model

We built a lightweight integration that feeds new feature requests into an LLM via an API webhook. But raw LLM processing isn't enough; it needs context. 

We configured the system to inject our **Quarterly OKRs** (Objectives and Key Results) and our current **Technical Debt Ledger** into the prompt alongside the new feature request.

### The Workflow Pipeline

Here is how the automated pipeline functions before a human PM ever looks at a ticket:

1. **Intake:** User submits feedback or a stakeholder requests a feature.
2. **LLM Enrichment:** The AI tags the request with related product domains (e.g., `Authentication`, `Checkout`).
3. **Sentiment & Priority Scoring:** The AI scores the request based on strategic alignment.
4. **Auto-Archiving:** Any ticket scoring below a 4 is automatically moved to an "Icebox" state with a polite automated note.

### Results and Human Oversight

While the AI is not perfect, it immediately flags the bottom 40% of noise. This allows the human Product Managers and Engineering Leads to focus exclusively on the top-tier ideas during grooming sessions. 

The goal isn't to replace the PM; the goal is to give the PM their time back so they can focus on strategy and customer interviews instead of ticket administration. By implementing this, we reduced grooming time from 4 hours a week to just 45 minutes.
