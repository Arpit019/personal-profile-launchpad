## Death of the DAU (Daily Active Users)

For the last decade, investors and product managers have obsessed over DAU (Daily Active Users) and MAU (Monthly Active Users). It looked great on a pitch deck. However, in modern product management, DAU is increasingly recognized as a vanity metric that masks poor retention and low product-market fit.

A user logging in just to clear a notification badge is technically "active", but they aren't extracting any real value. If your growth strategy relies on notification spam to boost DAU, you are building a leaky bucket.

### Measuring the "Aha!" Moment

In 2026, we must shift our focus to measuring **Core Actions**. 

Instead of tracking logins, you must track the specific action that heavily correlates with long-term retention. This is often referred to as the "Aha!" moment—the exact point a user realizes the value of your product.

*   **For a Gaming App:** It isn't opening the app; it's completing 3 multiplayer matches.
*   **For a B2B SaaS Tool:** It isn't creating an account; it's successfully inviting a team member and collaborating on a file.
*   **For an E-commerce Platform:** It isn't browsing items; it's adding a payment method.

### The Rise of Engagement Depth

Another critical metric is **Engagement Depth**. Are users utilizing the core features, or are they hovering around the edges of the product? 

We started tracking Feature Adoption Rates segmented by user cohorts. We discovered that users who engaged with our "Advanced Analytics" module had a 90% lower churn rate. Our product strategy immediately shifted from "acquire new users" to "funnel existing users into the Advanced Analytics module."

### Retention over Acquisition

Acquisition is expensive; retention is cheap. By moving away from DAU and focusing relentlessly on Core Actions and Time-to-Value, you align your product team's incentives with actual user success, rather than just marketing hype.
